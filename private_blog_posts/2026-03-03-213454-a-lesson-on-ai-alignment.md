title: "A lesson on AI Alignment"
date: "2026-03-03T21:34:54:z"
---

It is a folly to believe that just because an AI can perform better than humans, that humans need not apply. Chess Engines have long surpassed humans, but just regurgitating what the Engine tells us is a terrible way to improve. Using the Chess Engines as a tool can help players improve, but the real magic comes from what happens within the individuals' brain. 

Of course, the analogy is not one to one, since Chess economically hinges upon a preference for human players. It is meant to be a fun activity which we care about human excellence in. Whereas domains like Software Engineering are ultimately a means to an end for upper management. However, I don't think we can fully replace humans yet. 

The biggest factor is the aspect of human agency. In fact, if we inspect this further, we'll realize that human agency is the same thing as AI Alignment. If humans cannot understand the output of AI anymore, we cannot be fully aligned with it. As humans, we NEED to "build up our fundamentals" so to speak, or our knowledge of what we don't know will be increasingly small. 

There are domains that can be replaced with AI already, but that just means we need to expand our scope as humans. We don't need to focus on code syntax anymore. But having eclectic knowledge across multiple domains and the intuition to tie it all together is still very important. Because this action is still borne of human desire. It is still serving humans. 

Without this, we'd have no alignment.
